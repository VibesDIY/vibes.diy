For a long time the whole pitch was *make apps with your friends, so easy even AI can do it.* It's a good line. It's still true. But sit with it next to the weakest sentence in software marketing — *"we build apps"* — and you notice they're cousins. Both describe the **mechanism** (an app appears) and skip the **job** (the thing the app does for you). The strongest offer is never the noun. It's the verb.

So here's the reframe we're rolling through the site, starting with the [About page](https://vibes.diy/about): **from the group chat to the front counter.**

## Same move, both ends

Watch what someone actually does on Vibes and it's identical whether they're having fun or running a business:

> Describe it → it's live at a link → you keep changing it by talking to it → you decide who's in.

For the group chat that's a trivia night, a would-you-rather generator, a character bot that answers in your voice. For the front counter it's a daily-specials board that **takes the order and the payment**, a booking-and-intake form your clients fill out themselves, a phone-friendly inventory count, the shared spreadsheet your team fights over turned into a real app with a form and a dashboard.

Nobody changed tools between those two sentences. That's the whole point.

## The part we'd been too shy to say: money

Scroll our old copy and you'll find character bots, drum machines, painting apps — and not one mention of taking a payment. That's a strange omission, because *handling money* is the single most credible signal that a thing is a real tool and not a toy. The taco truck doesn't want an "app." It wants the order to land, the payment to clear, and the kitchen to see the queue. Vibes does that now, and the copy should have been saying so the whole time.

So it does. "Takes the order. Takes the payment." is a line we're putting front and center — right next to "impress the group chat," not instead of it.

## Why this doesn't cost us the soul

The fear with going after the spreadsheet-heavy small business is that you wake up sounding like every B2B SaaS landing page — *workflow automation, digital transformation, internal tooling*. That language is exactly the generic "we build apps" trap wearing a tie. We're not doing that. The voice doesn't change. We still say *kill the spreadsheet*, not *streamline your operational workflows.* We still say *run the front counter*, not *point-of-sale enablement.* Concrete and situation-shaped, every time.

And the one feature the serious operator needs most is something we already shipped for the group chat: **private by default, you choose who can read, write, and open it.** What reads as a social nicety for friends reads as table-stakes access control for anyone handling a customer's order. Same control. Two audiences. One sentence.

## What's changing

This post ships alongside copy edits across the front doors — the in-app About and home pages, the public good.vibes.diy hero, the README, the builder's field guide, and a fresh set of starter prompts (an order form, a specials board, a spreadsheet-killer, a booking app, an inventory count, a customer portal) so the very first thing you can tap proves it's a real tool.

Seeing a vibe should still make you want to change it. We're just widening *what* you might be looking at — from the bit your group chat will love to the thing that finally runs your shop.
