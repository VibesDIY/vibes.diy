# Available But Unharvested Course Collections

Surveyed May 2026. Documents collections suitable for Phase 1 harvest in a future run.
For pipeline context see `../agents/resource-harvest-to-pages.md`.

Two domains covered: **Humanities** (same 8 institutions as the academic-syllabi harvest) and **Media Arts** (a distinct domain with its own source set).

---

## Humanities — Same 8 Institutions

Not all universities publish syllabi openly. Accessibility varies widely.

### Tier 1 — Directly harvestable (full syllabi, no login)

**UT Austin — HB2504 Mandatory Public Repository**
Texas House Bill 2504 (2009) requires every undergraduate course with 5+ students to publish its syllabus within 7 days of the semester start. The archive is live at https://utdirect.utexas.edu/apps/student/coursedocs/nlogon/ — no login, downloadable PDFs, filterable by department code, semester, and instructor. Covers the full humanities span: English (E), History (HIS), Philosophy (PHI), Classics (CC), Linguistics (LIN), Art History (ARH), Music (MUS), Theatre (TD), Film Studies (RTF), Religious Studies (RS). Scale: ~10,000–12,000 undergraduate sections per year institution-wide, several hundred humanities sections per semester. Archive goes back multiple years. This is the largest single open syllabus collection at any of the 8 harvested institutions.

**MIT OCW — Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences**
The MIT SHASS contribution to OCW is ~800 courses with full syllabi — reading lists, week-by-week schedules, grading breakdowns, assignment descriptions. No login required. Departments: Literature, History (~70 subjects from Ancient to Contemporary), Linguistics and Philosophy, Music and Theater Arts, Comparative Media Studies/Writing, Art History (within Architecture). The American Literature course (21L.006), for example, has a full 26-session calendar with thematic unit labels and all graded work enumerated — this is full disclosure, not a course description stub.

Entry points: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/history/ · https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/literature/

**UW–Madison — History Department Syllabi Library**
Dedicated, structured archive at https://history.wisc.edu/syllabi-library/ organized by course-number range (100-level through 900-level doctoral seminars). Individual course pages host PDF syllabi from multiple semesters and instructors. ~50–100 distinct course entries. Actual documents, no login.

Also: Integrated Liberal Studies (ILS 201–206 humanities core sequences) at https://ils.wisc.edu/course-syllabi/ — 2 pages of entries covering literature, art, and intellectual history interdisciplinary sequences.

### Tier 2 — Partially accessible (some departments open, others gated)

**UC Berkeley — Linguistics, Sociology, Geography archives**
No central humanities syllabus repository. Three departments have dedicated open PDF archives:
- Linguistics: https://lx.berkeley.edu/academics/undergraduate-program/linguistics-course-syllabi-archive
- Sociology: https://sociology.berkeley.edu/resources/syllabi-main-page (~10–20 PDFs per term, back to Fall 2023)
- Geography: https://geography.berkeley.edu/course-syllabi (~15–20 courses)

English, History, Philosophy, Film & Media, Music, and Art History post course descriptions only; syllabi are in bCourses (gated). Berkeley's Class Search (classes.berkeley.edu) covers ~5,900 Arts & Literature and ~3,700 Historical Studies courses with descriptions but no attached syllabi.

### Tier 3 — Not yet accessible (but watch list)

**UNC Chapel Hill**
The UNC System adopted a regulation in December 2025 treating all syllabi as public records effective January 15, 2026, and requiring each campus to build a "readily searchable" public platform before Fall 2026. As of May 2026 that platform is not yet live. Syllabi are legally obtainable via public records request, but no self-service URL exists. Individual department pages post some syllabi behind ONYEN login. Check back Fall 2026.

Reference: https://wunc.org/education/2025-12-20/its-official-north-carolina-professors-will-have-to-publicly-post-syllabi

**Stanford**
No OCW equivalent for humanities. ExploreCourses and the Canvas syllabus portal (syllabus.stanford.edu) both require Stanford authentication. A small number of courses are free-auditable via Coursera/edX but syllabi are not downloadable. Contrast with MIT OCW is stark.

**CMU (Dietrich College)**
University-wide Syllabus Registry at https://www.cmu.edu/syllabusregistry/ requires Andrew ID. Dietrich departments (English, History, Philosophy, Linguistics) post titles and descriptions only. School of Music has a few publicly posted PDFs as an exception.

**UW Seattle**
English department posts sample syllabi for writing courses (2–3 documents) at https://english.washington.edu/teaching/sample-syllabi-0. All other humanities departments — Philosophy, Cinema & Media Studies, Drama, Art History, Music — post course descriptions only via Canvas.

## Cross-Institutional Humanities Collections

These span multiple institutions and are worth surveying before a dedicated humanities harvest run.

### H-Net (h-net.org) — **immediately harvestable**
Decentralized archives across 180+ discipline-specific scholarly networks, overwhelmingly history and related humanities. Rich, openly accessible full-text syllabi — real course documents with reading lists, assignment descriptions, weekly schedules — contributed by member scholars, no login. Scale: hundreds to low thousands per active network, tens of thousands site-wide.

Strong networks with syllabus archives:
- H-Diplo (diplomatic/international history): https://networks.h-net.org/node/28443/pages/59076/h-diplo-syllabus-archive
- H-Urban (urban history): https://networks.h-net.org/node/22277/pages/40096/syllabus-archive-introduction
- H-LatAm (Latin American history, joint with CLAH): https://networks.h-net.org/node/23910/pages/27764/h-latam-syllabi
- HABSBURG (Habsburg/Central European): https://networks.h-net.org/node/19389/pages/823612/syllabi-database
- Also: H-German, H-Slavery, H-Buddhism, H-Empire

### Humanities Commons CORE — **immediately harvestable**
Scholar-deposited open-access repository at Michigan State. Syllabi are a depositable type alongside articles and course materials. Covers literary studies, comparative literature, history, philosophy, film/media, queer theory, Jewish studies, digital humanities. Each deposit gets a DOI, fully downloadable, no login. Scale: hundreds of syllabi — useful for qualitative depth rather than mass coverage.

Entry: https://hcommons.org/deposits/?facets%5Bgenre_facet%5D%5B%5D=Syllabus

### Health Humanities Syllabus Repository (Rice University)
Focused niche: literature and medicine, history of medicine, bioethics, disability studies, STS, medical anthropology, film and health. Multi-institutional, searchable by topic/discipline/course level. Full syllabus text viewable. Scale: hundreds of syllabi.

Entry: https://healthhumanitiessyllabi.rice.edu/

### Open Syllabus Project — **research license required**
15.7 million syllabi, 4,000+ institutions, 25 billion words of full text. CIP-classified into 62 subject fields. Humanities coverage is strong: English/Literature (~1M syllabi), History/Political Science (~500–700K), Philosophy (top texts: Aristotle, Mill, Plato), Religious Studies. Full-text download requires a formal research license (university affiliation + use agreement). The free Syllabus Explorer (analytics.opensyllabus.org) surfaces ranked book/article assignments but caps data at 2019 and hides individual syllabi.

Contact: [email protected] · Docs: https://docs.opensyllabus.org

## Media Arts — New Domain

This is a distinct domain from academic humanities. The hinge document is the **course website** or **GitHub-hosted syllabus** rather than a PDF on a department page. Creators are media arts faculty and practitioners; audience is creative technologists, artists, and engineers who search for specific techniques (physical computing, p5.js, computer vision, sound synthesis).

### Tier 1 — Directly harvestable

**NYU ITP / IMA — the richest single source**
Three parallel channels, all public and deep:

1. **itp.nyu.edu/classes/listings** — WordPress index of hundreds of ITP/IMA course websites tagged by topic (sound, video, 3d, fabrication, coding, internet, game, pcomp, networked media). No login. Examples: "Immersive Listening: Designing Sound for VR," "Live Web," "Networked Media," "Cloud Commuting." Coverage: roughly 2008–present.

2. **github.com/ITPNYU** (117 repos) — ICM (Introduction to Computational Media) repos for every year 2015–2025, both Code and Media tracks; Physical Computing lab materials; ML for Physical Computing. All week-by-week Markdown syllabi.

3. **github.com/IDMNYU** (198 repos) — NYU Tandon Integrated Design & Media: Creative Coding, Sound Studio, Motion Graphics, Advanced Creative Coding, Thesis courses.

4. **itp.nyu.edu/physcomp/** — dedicated Physical Computing microsite, full syllabus archive back to Fall 2025, per-section instructor pages.

Per-topic tag URLs: `itp.nyu.edu/classes/listings/tag/sound/`, `.../tag/3d/`, `.../tag/coding/`

**UCLA Design Media Arts — classes.dma.ucla.edu**
Public archive of individual course websites from ~2007–2023, organized by term and course number. Full syllabi, schedules, assignments, and student work for many offerings. Subjects confirmed: DESMA 9 (art & science survey), DESMA 28 (interactivity/programming intro), DESMA 111 (art and the internet), DESMA 113 (generative art), DESMA 161 (physical computing/sound), DESMA 252A/B (Programming Media I/II — Arduino, software, 3D), Sound as Material (160), Cultural Automation with Machine Learning. High hit rate for core courses.

**CMU — Golan Levin's course archive**
golancourses.net (2004–present) and ems.andrew.cmu.edu (2009–2022): 15–20 distinct courses with full syllabi, daily notes, assignments, and student work. Exceptional depth for a single-faculty archive. Courses: 60-212 Interactivity & Computation (p5.js, Processing, Arduino, Unity), Drawing with Machines, Experimental Capture, Interactive Art (60-423), graduate seminars. Companion GitHub: github.com/golanlevin/DrawingWithMachines.

Not institutionally maintained but more complete than CMU's official channels.

**SFPC (School for Poetic Computation)**
sfpc.study/classes and legacy sfpc.io: full week-by-week course pages openly accessible, no login. GitHub org (github.com/sfpc, 49 repos) hosts supplementary materials. Subjects: Code Societies (software ideology + critical theory), Recreating the Past (algorithmic re-creation of historical art), Teaching as Art, Dark Matters (technology and race), Poetic Hardware (physical computing), Narrative Constellations (location-based storytelling). Small scale (10–15 active courses at a time) but opinionated, deep, and politically distinct from ITP/UCLA.

**MIT OCW — Media Arts and Sciences**
~35–40 MAS-numbered courses with full syllabi, readings, and assignments at https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/media-arts-and-sciences/. Older snapshots (mostly 2001–2011) but complete. Covers: computational media design (MAS.110), sensor technologies for interactive environments (MAS.836), holographic imaging (MAS.450), pattern recognition (MAS.622J), tangible interfaces, learning/creativity research.

### Tier 2 — Partially accessible

**RISD Digital + Media**
digitalmedia.risd.edu/syllabus: ~10–20 full syllabi for MFA courses in physical computing, robotics, and new media theory. Paul Badger's Physical Computing and Robotics wikis are completely open. Small scale but graduate-level depth.

**p5.js / Processing Community**
p5js.org/teach/ and p5js.org/education-resources/ aggregate community-contributed materials. Most substantive: NYC DOE CS4All ICM curriculum — full NYS Standards-aligned high-school course using p5.js (drawing/animation, interaction, functions, data, sound/video, ML), hosted at nycdoe-cs4all.github.io and cs4all-icm.gitbook.io.
